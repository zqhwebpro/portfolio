Animals

Bighorn Sheep

Though North America’s population of bighorn sheep suffered losses in the thousands due to disease, they can still be found in California, Arizona, Washington, Utah, Arizona, New Mexico, Texas, Wyoming, North & South Dakota, Montana, and Colorado.

Grizzly Bears

Known to scientists as the North American brown bear, grizzly bears are primarily found in Alaska, Montana, Wyoming, and Idaho.

Mountain Lions

Found throughout the West region of the US, the cougar is the largest cat in North America.

Great Grey Owl

This owl is the largest species of owl (based on length) and is found primarily in Oregon and California. It’s impressive wingspan and size make it a worthwhile sight to see for the avid birdwatcher.

Mustang

Spread across the American west, the mustang is often referred to as a wild horse, or a feral horse due to its descendancy from once-domesticated horses.

Red Fox

The red fox, known for their impressive cunning and intelligence and is the largest true fox and can be found in the western montane portions of North America.
