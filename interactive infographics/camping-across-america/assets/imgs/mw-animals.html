Animals

Bison

American Bison are common in the Great Plains, particularly in North and South Dakota. Bison were almost hunted to extinction but conservation efforts have brought them back from the brink.

Prairie Dogs

Burrowing rodents found throughout the Midwest that are herbivorous and named after their warning call that sounds like a bark. Their small size and large numbers makes them a common sight in the region.

Elk

Male elk that can be found in the Midwest shed their antlers once a year and display ritualized mating behaviors during the rut.

White Pelicans

During the summer, these pelicans migrate through the midwest--the only species of pelican to do this. This makes them a somewhat unexpected seasonal sight in the region.

Golden Eagles

A seasonal favorite during the winter for bird watchers and tourists in the blufflands of Minnesota, Wisconsin, and Iowa, these eagles nest as they wait out the season.

Gray Wolves

In the Great Lakes are alone there are over 3,500 gray wolves inhabiting the area. Outside of Minnesota, Michigan, and Wisconsin, gray wolves can be found in other midwest states as well.
