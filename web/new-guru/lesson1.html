HTML5 Semantics Tags

Objective:

In lesson one we will teach you what type of HTML5 Semantics there are and also how to use these various types of HTML5 Semantics tags. It is in our intent to take beginners of HTML and turn them into intermediate users. HTML5 semantics tags are industry standard labeling practices done by everyone in the industry.

Prerequisites:

<footer>

<header>

The header tag is where a web developer generally place their logos, headlines, and navagational items.

<footer>

The footer is where contact information, social media links, or copyright information is located.

<nav>

The nav is where a web developer would place all of their links to navigate through the website.

<article>

If you have content that is considered to be an article of information or it is information independent from the rest of the content, then you must use the article tag as the proper means of labeling.

<section>

In order to divide content by sections, chapters, or whatever other types of section division is on your website use the section tag.

<time>

The time tag signifies a moment of time. There are also two attributes associated with this tag one is datetime and one is pubtime. Below are examples of each.

<aside>

Whenever you have content to add text to that explains something to do with the content, then you can use the aside tag.

<mark>

Marked or highlighted text defined by the mark tag will help a user to identify specific text on your webpage.

<figure> & <figcaption>

The figure tag is used for self-contained content, the best example being images but you can also use figure for charts, self-contained div content, etc. If you add the figcaption tag then you can caption the self contained information..
