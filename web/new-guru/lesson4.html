Objective:

This module is designed to teach you what a CAPTCHA is and how to use Googles' reCAPTCHA on a website.

Sub-objectives:

New-guru will teach you how to place a Google reCAPTCHA on a website step by step.

You will also learn some basic PHP.

Prerequisites:

Basic knowledge of HTML and CSS are required to fully comprehend this lesson. PHP experience a benefit, but not necessary.


What is a CAPTCHA? What is the benefit of using Google's reCAPTCHA?

CAPTCHA stands for Completely Automated Public Turing test to tell Computers and Humans Apart. Basically though, it's placed onto websites to protect against robots and unwanted spam. You might remember them as those hard to read captions of words on the form portion of the webpage, since before though they've gotten simpler to use. Google has designed their very own version of CAPTCHA called reCAPTCHA. reCAPTCHA is a secure form that learns behaviors and adjust access depending on whether you are a human or a bot.

Tutorial 4 - Using a reCAPTCHA.

In the following tutorial you will learn how to add a reCAPTCHA of your choice to any website. So open up your favorite web development software, open up your browser, and get ready to learn how to use a Google reCAPTCHA.

Step 1.: Getting your sites key

First order of business, you will need to go over to the Google reCAPTCHA website: Here to register a site to receive your sites individually assigned site key. So click the link, then click the blue "Get reCAPTCHA" button to register a new site. The label is what you would like to be called and the domain is the websites domain address that you will be attaching the reCAPTCHA to.

Step 2.: Client-side integration

Now that you have registered your site, the next screen will show your new personal site keys, step 1 for client-side integration. Follow Step 1 by pasting the code given to you in the header and pasting the div class of "g-reCAPTCHA" at the end of the form associated with this reCAPTCHA. Don't have a form yet? You can borrow mine.
