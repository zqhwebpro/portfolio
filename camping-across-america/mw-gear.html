Temperatures can fall well below zero in various parts of the region, though it isn’t always typical of the midwest, which is why it’s essential to prepare for extreme changes in weather, regardless of the season.

  • Pack Layers Of Clothing*
  • Winter Clothes
  • Snow Pants
  • Winter-Ready Tent and Bleeping Bags

Spring tends to be moderate in weather, but because of how varied the weather can be from state to state, it’s advised to come prepared for highs and lows.

  • Pack Layers Of Clothing*, Like Lighter Clothes In Case of a Heatwave
  • Long Pants, Flannel Shirts, and Sweaters
  • Emergency Umbrella
  • Three-Season Tent & Sleeping Bag

Like spring, autumn sees moderate temperatures but campers should be prepared for higher or lower than expected averages.

  • Pack Layers of Clothing*, but Mostly Shorts and T-Shirts
  • Rain Gear and Waterproof Hiking Boots
  • Windbreaker
  • Extra Blankets in Case of Low Temperatures
