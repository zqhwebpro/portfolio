Cape Cod National Seashore

Covering 43,607 acres on Cape Cod, in Massachusetts, the Cape Cod National Seashore was created by President Kennedy in 1961 and features the Marconi Station--the site of the first two-way transatlantic radio transmission.

Acadia National Park

Located in Maine and southwest of Bar Harbor, the park preserves nearly half of Mount Desert Island, along with several other nearby, smaller islands. The park’s original name was Sieur de Monts National Monument, give by President Wilson.

The forest of Allegheny National Park covers 513,175 acres and is located in northwestern Pennsylvania. The first commercial oil well site is located just 40 miles from the Forest, as well.
