Animals

Black Bear

One of the world’s most common bear species, the American Black Bear can be found throughout New Jersey, New York, Vermont, Pennsylvania, and Maine. Black bears are commonly hunted throughout parts of the North and New England area.

White-Tailed Deer

The white-tailed deer is the state animal of Pennsylvania and New Hampshire, and can be found through those states. They’re common game for PA hunters as well.

Coyote

Of the Coyotes found throughout Pennsylvania, New England, and New York, it’s likely that a portion of those responsible for attacks are actually coywolves--wolf and coyote hybrids.

Eastern Moose

Found in New York, Vermont, New Hampshire, and Connecticut, the Eastern Moose is the third largest native North American subspecies to be found on the continent.

Canada Lync

Formerly occupying a much wider territory and habitat, the Canada Lynx population is now mostly concentrated in Maine and throughout the New England area and forests of northern states.

Osprey

Bird of prey found throughout Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and along the Eastern Coast; but can also be found in every other continent except for Antarctica. The Osprey is an impressive sight during flight.
